KSU & CARIT: Opening Doors for the Next Generation of Tech Leaders

Kennesaw State University has long positioned itself as a university of opportunity, and nowhere is that more visible than through the Center for Applied Research in Information Technology (CARIT). Designed to bridge the gap between classroom learning and real-world experience, CARIT offers students hands-on research positions, collaborative projects with industry partners, and a launchpad for careers in data, cybersecurity, web development, and beyond.

Student assistant positions at CARIT are more than just resume builders - they are immersive experiences that place students alongside faculty researchers tackling real problems. Whether analyzing data pipelines, developing web tools, or contributing to ongoing studies, CARIT students graduate with a portfolio that speaks for itself.

Beyond paid positions, KSU encourages students to engage through volunteer and service-learning opportunities embedded across its colleges. From tech outreach programs in the greater Atlanta metro area to open-source contribution initiatives, there is no shortage of ways to get involved. KSU's commitment to access means that opportunity is not reserved for the few - it is engineered into the institution itself.

Cyborg Botany: Augmented plants as sensors, displays, and actuators

Researchers at MIT are exploring "Cyborg Botany" - merging living plants with electronic technology. Plants already communicate through color, moisture, and position, and even carry internal electrochemical signals similar to circuits. The project taps into these natural capabilities to create organic interfaces that move human-computer interaction off screens and back into the natural world.
